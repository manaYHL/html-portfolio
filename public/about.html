About Me

Statement

I was born and raised in Northeast China, graduated from the University of International Business and Economics with an International Politics major. Like a lot of other Asian young people, I had never thought about what kind of career I wanted to have in the future, since the only goal for me back then was getting admitted to a top-tier university, thinking that I would naturally get a great job after the graduation. That's what I was told by my parents and school teachers.

However, I struggled a lot with my study in the university because I didn't like my major at all. More importantly, I didn't even know where my passion lied and what I was good at. Then, I came to Korea, fumbled around in the dark for two years, did various types of part-time jobs. However, I was not satisfied with the financial and social status back then. So I decided to apply for a graduate program, exactly knowing what I didn't like but still not knowing what I felt passionate about. Since I was exposed to a lot of business and financial information during my university, I decided to challenge for the finance major. That's the only area that I was relatively familiar with other than the International Politics.

Surprisingly, I got admitted to the program and did a quite decent job. During the time, I got to know about data science, and about programming. And I immediately got mesmerized by the power and the insight I could get out of the tools such as R, Python, and Java. After graduation, I was able to get an investment related job. By getting lots of second-hand information of how corporations running their businesses, I started to realize that if I want to thrive then I have to know better about computer and technology. That's why I decided to learn more deeply about programming and am very sure that I would do a good job. Now I am very certain about what I want to do and achieve, because I know that I would be good in the fintech domain given the background, talent, and determination that I have now.
